# Best AI Tool for Interior Design Renders

Interior designers need AI that understands rooms, not just images. Most generic tools fail because they don't get spatial relationships, material logic, or lighting principles.

## Why Generic Tools Fail

**The Problem:**
```text
Generic AI sees: "A room with stuff in it"
Interior designer needs: "A 12x15 living room with proper 
furniture scale, realistic materials, and natural lighting"
```

**What goes wrong:**
- Distorted room dimensions (rooms become impossible shapes)
- Material chaos (wood on ceilings, fabric on floors)
- Lighting nightmares (light sources that don't exist)
- Spatial confusion (furniture that doesn't fit)

## Best Practices

### 1. Start with Clear Inputs

```text
Good input = Good output
Bad input = Inconsistent mess
```

Clear sketches or photos produce better renders. Blurry, low-res images = disappointing results.

### 2. Be Specific About Materials

**Bad:**
```text
"Make it look nice"
```

**Good:**
```text
"Light oak hardwood floors, white painted walls, 
natural linen curtains, mid-century modern furniture"
```

### 3. Use Reference Images

Upload material samples or style references:

```text
Upload: Wood sample image
Prompt: "Apply this wood texture to floors" → @latest
Result: Consistent material application
```

### 4. Leverage Render Chains

Build on previous renders:

```text
v1: Initial concept
v2: "Add plants and natural elements" → @v1
v3: "Make lighting warmer" → @v2
v4: "Refine furniture placement" → @v3

All tracked automatically in render chain
```
